# Platform Tone — Voice Adaptation Rules

## Reference: [[voice/brand-voice]] is the source DNA. This file adapts it per platform.

## X (Twitter) — Primary Platform

**Energy:** Direct, punchy, data-first. Like a sharp research note from a friend, not a presentation.
**Register:** Casual-professional. Lowercase acceptable for emphasis. Never all-caps except in rare single-word emphasis.
**Sentence length:** Max 15 words per sentence. Most should be 5-10.
**Paragraph style:** 1-2 sentences per block. Whitespace between every thought.
**Personality dial:** 70% analytic, 20% personal/vulnerable, 10% dry humor
**Thread voice:** Each tweet must stand alone AND connect. No "1/7" numbering — let the content flow.

### X-Specific Adaptations
- Hook is sharper than any other platform — must stop a scroll in <1 second
- Numbers always bold or at the front of the line
- Confession/vulnerability hits harder here than on any other platform
- Technical depth is OK — the audience is sophisticated
- Self-reply with additional context, links, disclosures

## Substack / Newsletter (Future)

**Energy:** Expansive, reflective. The long version of what X gets in compressed form.
**Register:** Conversational but thorough. Like a letter to a smart friend.
**Sentence length:** Variable. Mix short punches with longer explanatory sentences.
**Personality dial:** 40% analytic, 40% personal/reflective, 20% educational

## Instagram (Future, if applicable)

**Energy:** Visual-first. Text supports the image, not the other way around.
**Register:** Clean, minimal text. Let screenshots and charts do the talking.

## General Rule

Same person at a research meetup (X), writing a journal entry (Newsletter), and showing a friend their screen (Instagram). The core identity from [[voice/brand-voice]] never changes — only the delivery adapts.
