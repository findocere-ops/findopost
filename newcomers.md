# Audience: Newcomers (Crypto-Curious, Zero Knowledge)

## Who They Are

- Have heard of Bitcoin/crypto but never used DeFi
- May be tech-savvy in other domains (web dev, finance, gaming)
- Intimidated by jargon: IL, TVL, LP, AMM mean nothing to them
- Looking for honest entry points, not get-rich-quick schemes
- Often found via viral posts that cross into mainstream X feeds

## What They Care About

- "Is this real or a scam?" — trust is the #1 barrier
- Simple explanations without condescension
- Real numbers from real people (not theoretical charts)
- Risk honesty — they want to know what can go wrong
- Entry points: what's the minimum to start, what tools do I need

## What Language Lands

DO:
- Analogy-first: "DLMM bins are like shelf positions — the closer to where people are shopping, the more you earn"
- Numbers with context: "+3.4% in 30 days, which is better than a savings account but worse than holding SOL"
- Honest risk framing: "I could have lost X if the market moved Y"
- Show the journey: "6 months ago I didn't know what IL meant either"

DON'T:
- Assume knowledge of any DeFi term without brief context
- Use insider slang: "degen", "ape in", "LFG"
- Promise returns or downplay risks
- Gatekeep: "if you don't know this, you shouldn't be here"

## How They Engage

- Like and bookmark but rarely reply (lurkers)
- Follow if the account feels trustworthy and non-intimidating
- Share posts that demystify crypto to their non-crypto friends
- Eventually DM asking "where do I start?"

## Content That Converts This Audience

- Posts that go viral because they're accessible without being dumbed down
- "Everything I know about X — a thread" compilation posts
- Honest annual return comparisons (DeFi vs. savings vs. stocks)
- The "one SOL and million dreams" origin story — relatable underdog narrative

## Integration Note

Newcomer-accessible content should be ~10% of output. Most posts target practitioners. But occasional anchor posts should be written broadly enough that a newcomer can follow without feeling lost.
