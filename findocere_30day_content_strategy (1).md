## 2. Voice Rules

1. **First person, present tense.** Write from inside the position, not looking back at it. "I'm watching this pool bleed fees" not "I observed that the pool lost fees."
2. **Numbers before narrative.** Lead with the data point. The story earns its place after the metric lands.
3. **No external links in post body.** Links go in the first reply only. This is an algorithm survival rule — treat it as law.
4. **0–1 hashtags maximum.** Never hashtag-stack. If you must, one — and only if it's a live event tag like #SuperteamINDO.
5. **Never position as expert or guru.** Use framing like "here's what I found" / "this surprised me" / "I got this wrong." The persona is: practitioner who learned the hard way.
6. **Short sentences, generous whitespace.** Every line break is a micro-decision to keep reading. Walls of text are scroll fuel — for the wrong direction.
7. **Emoji as punctuation, not decoration.** One per post max, functional only (📊 for data, ⚠️ for risk). Never string multiple emoji.
8. **End with a question that invites opinion, not agreement.** "What's your take?" is lazy. "Would you hold this through a 15% SOL dump?" forces thought.
9. **Disclose positions always.** If you're in the pool, say so. If you exited, say when. Transparency is the moat.
10. **Mix English and Indonesian sparingly.** English-primary for reach. Indonesian flavor ("om", "bang") only in replies and community threads — never in hooks.

## 3. Hook Formula Library

### H1 — The Confession Hook
**Trigger:** Vulnerability + specificity → trust + curiosity  
**Template:** "I [did/believed X]. It cost me [specific consequence]."  
**Example:** "I held my DLMM position through a 20% SOL drop. Here's what it actually cost me."  
**Best for:** Single tweets, journal posts. Maximizes replies (people share their own losses).

**Trigger:** Pattern interruption → confusion → scroll stop  
**Template:** "[Common belief] is wrong. Here's what actually happens."  
**Example:** "Higher APY doesn't mean higher profit. Most DLMM farmers lose money on the best-looking pools."  
**Best for:** Threads (claim → proof → framework). Maximizes bookmarks.

**Trigger:** Urgency + freshness → FOMO + relevance  
**Template:** "[Time marker]: [unexpected observation from real data]."  
**Example:** "Last 72 hours: stablecoin LP yields on Meteora quietly doubled. Nobody's talking about why."  
**Best for:** Single tweets, alpha drops. Maximizes early engagement velocity.

**Example:** "+6.2% in 11 days on a stablecoin pair. No leverage, no degen plays. Just bin management."

**Example:** "Nobody is watching Meteora's DAMM V2 fee structure. The edge is in the bin spacing."  
**Best for:** Threads. Maximizes bookmarks + reply-debates.

**Trigger:** Changed mind + intellectual honesty → respect + engagement

### H8 — The Question-as-Hook
**Trigger:** Direct challenge to reader → ego activation → reply  
**Template:** "[Provocative question that assumes the reader is doing something suboptimal]?"  
**Example:** "If your LP strategy can't survive a 15% drawdown, do you actually have a strategy?"

### ANCHOR 1 (Day 7): How DLMM bin spacing actually determines your profit

**Tweet 1/6:**  
How DLMM bin spacing actually determines your profit — not APY.

Most Meteora farmers pick pools by APY.

That's like choosing a restaurant by how big the sign is. 🧵

**Tweet 2/6:**  
Quick primer: DLMM pools divide the price range into discrete "bins." Each bin is a tiny concentrated position.

Wider bin spacing = your liquidity is spread across more price range.  
Tighter bin spacing = your liquidity is concentrated in a narrow band.

The APY dashboard doesn't tell you which setup actually captures more fees for YOUR situation.

**Tweet 3/6:**  
I ran two positions side by side for 14 days:

Position A: 45% displayed APY, wide bins (50 bins across ±10% range)  
Position B: 18% displayed APY, tight bins (15 bins across ±2% range)

Result: Position B earned 3.1x more fees per dollar of liquidity.

Why? Because SOL traded within a 3% range for 11 of those 14 days. The tight bins captured nearly every trade. The wide bins mostly sat idle.

**Tweet 4/6:**  
The takeaway isn't "always use tight bins."

It's: match your bin spacing to expected volatility.

Low-volatility period → tight bins, lower displayed APY, higher actual fee capture  
High-volatility period → wider bins, because tight bins get run over and you eat IL

**Tweet 5/6:**  
My actual workflow before entering any DLMM position:

1. Check 7-day price range on the pair (not 24h — too noisy)  
2. Set bins to cover ~1.5x that range  
3. If realized vol is < 3%, go tight. If > 8%, go wide or sit out  
4. Recheck every 3 days — if vol regime changed, adjust

Takes 4 minutes. Saves weeks of dead capital.

**Tweet 6/6:**  
This is the stuff that actually matters for DLMM profitability. Not the APY number. Not the TVL. The bin configuration relative to current volatility.

Save this for your next position setup.

What's your approach to bin spacing? Do you set and forget, or actively manage? 👇
